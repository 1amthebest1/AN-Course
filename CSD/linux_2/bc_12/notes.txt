A cron job is a scheduled task in Linux and Unix systems. It's like telling your computer:

"Run this command automatically at a specific time or interval."

The service that executes these scheduled tasks is called cron, and the list of scheduled tasks is stored in a crontab (cron table).

Crontab Format

Each line in a crontab follows this format:

* * * * * command_to_run
│ │ │ │ │
│ │ │ │ └── Day of week (0-7) (Sunday = 0 or 7)
│ │ │ └──── Month (1-12)
│ │ └────── Day of month (1-31)
│ └──────── Hour (0-23)
└────────── Minute (0-59)

Example:

30 14 * * * /home/user/backup.sh

This means:

Minute: 30
Hour: 14 (2 PM)
Day of month: * (every day)
Month: * (every month)
Day of week: * (every day of the week)

Runs every day at 2:30 PM.

Special Characters
Asterisk (*)

Means "every".

* * * * *

Runs every minute.

Comma (,)
0 9,17 * * *

Runs at 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM.

Dash (-)
0 9-17 * * *

Runs every hour from 9 AM through 5 PM.

Slash (/)
*/15 * * * *

Runs every 15 minutes:

00,15,30,45
Reading Examples
0 0 * * *

→ Every day at midnight.

0 8 * * 1-5

→ Every weekday at 8:00 AM.

*/10 * * * *

→ Every 10 minutes.

15 3 1 * *

→ At 3:15 AM on the 1st day of every month.

0 */2 * * *

→ Every 2 hours.

How to View Your Cron Jobs

Show your current user's cron jobs:

crontab -l

Edit your cron jobs:

crontab -e

Remove all cron jobs:

crontab -r
Set a Cron Job That Runs Every Hour

Open the crontab:

crontab -e

Add:

0 * * * * /path/to/script.sh

This means:

Minute: 0
Hour: every hour
Day: every day
Month: every month
Weekday: every weekday

It runs at:

01:00
02:00
03:00
...
23:00
00:00
Example

Suppose you have:

/home/ubuntu/backup.sh

Make it executable:

chmod +x /home/ubuntu/backup.sh

Then add:

0 * * * * /home/ubuntu/backup.sh

Cron will execute it at the beginning of every hour.

Redirect Output to a Log File
0 * * * * /home/ubuntu/backup.sh >> /var/log/backup.log 2>&1
>> appends standard output to the log.
2>&1 redirects errors into the same log file.
Useful Cron Shortcuts
@reboot    Run once after boot
@hourly    Run every hour
@daily     Run once a day
@weekly    Run once a week
@monthly   Run once a month
@yearly    Run once a year

Your hourly job could also be written as:

@hourly /home/ubuntu/backup.sh
TL;DR
Cron = Linux scheduler for automatic tasks.
Crontab line format:
minute hour day-of-month month day-of-week command
* means "every".
Edit cron jobs with:
crontab -e
View them with:
crontab -l
Run a script every hour:
0 * * * * /path/to/script.sh

or:

@hourly /path/to/script.sh
Make scripts executable and preferably log their output for debugging.
